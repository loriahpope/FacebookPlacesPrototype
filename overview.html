Facebook Places Concept - Desktop and Mobile

Category

Interaction Design

Inspiration

For years, Facebook has been a leader in interconnectivity between friends around the world. A missed opportunity exists in Places - users can get recommendations from their friends for restaurants, attractions, and more. I decided to give a light refresh to the Places page, to highlight what I believe to be key features in the application.

Heuristic Review

Landing Page


The landing page is inviting, with large images and text. It would be nice to be able to search through a filter for a more tailored experience (i.e. Eat > Mexican Food). The page navigaion is out of the main frame of the page, and is inconsistent with other pages on the site.

Problem Definition

The existing Facebook Places site does a great job of presenting information to the user, and using large, bright images to keep the users' attention. The pages themselves could use more focus, by making sure all of the pertinent information is readily available on desktop and on mobile. Some of the pages are busy, with lots of potentially distracting links and images on the page. Furthermore, adding a different navigation and more filter options may help to engage the user as well.

Upcoming Events


The highlighted events do not seem targeted toward the user, despite being under the 'Recommended' tab. More focus could be given to this section to let the user know what their friends and connections are doing in the area.

Friends' Places


This could be highlited on the page so users know what their friends are doing and where they could be going.

Popular Nearby


The map element is definitely helpful on this page, but it seems out of place unless it focuses on the area the user selected. Presumably, most users on this page are looking for things to do in a particular area, not in different cities.

Specific Event


There is a lot of information on this page. It's difficult for the user to understand what the focus of the page actually is. The important items on the page should answer the What, Where, and When of the event, with the Who (keeping Facebook's mission of connectiveness in mind) prominent on the page. Only information about the event should be on the main page, with supporting information being contained on external Facebook pages in an effort to keep the page focused. This is even more important on mobile, where the user must obtain a lot of information in limited space.
